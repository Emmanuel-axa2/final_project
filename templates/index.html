Pipeline

How it works under the hood

01

Your content comes in

You fill in a form or upload a file. The system reads PDF and Word documents automatically to extract your CV text — nothing is stored, it only lives in memory while you're using the tool.

02

The AI scores and analyses

If you provide a job description, two scoring methods run in parallel — one measures how similar the language is (TF-IDF), another checks for specific keyword matches. Both scores are blended into a single result.

03

Improvements are generated

The AI rewrites your CV sections using the analysis as context, returning the improved text alongside a breakdown of what was changed, what weaknesses were fixed, and a confidence rating for the output.

NOTE

The AI will never invent jobs, qualifications, or achievements you didn't provide. It is explicitly instructed to only rewrite and reframe what you give it — improving clarity and impact, not fabricating content.

Documentation

Frequently asked questions

Is this tool free to use?
Yes, completely. This is a final-year university project built for research and evaluation — there's no payment, no subscription, and no catch.
Is my CV saved anywhere?
No. Your CV text is only kept in memory while your browser session is active. It's sent to an AI model (via Groq's API) to generate improvements, but nothing is written to a database or stored on a server. When you close the tab or click "Start Fresh", it's gone.
How does the job match score work?
The system runs two checks at once. First, it measures how similar the language in your CV is to the job description using a technique called TF-IDF — think of it as comparing the vocabulary overlap mathematically. Second, the AI extracts specific keywords from the job posting and checks how many appear in your CV. Both results are combined into a single score out of 100.
Will the AI make things up about me?
No. The AI is explicitly instructed not to invent experience, qualifications, or achievements. It can only rewrite and improve what you've already provided — making your real experience sound clearer and more compelling.
Who is this for?
Primarily students, recent graduates, and early-career applicants looking for internships or entry-level roles — especially in technical fields where matching your CV to a job description matters.
What file types can I upload?
You can upload .pdf, .docx (Word), or .txt files. If your PDF is a scanned image rather than real text, the extraction won't work — paste your CV as plain text instead.
